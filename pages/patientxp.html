Flywire Patient Experience

For over 3 years I have been the product design lead on this project that lies at the intersection of Healthcare and FinTech.

What is it?

The patient experience is a consumer facing platform for patients to manage and pay their healthcare bills. It is where billing notifications are managed, as well as patient info and insurance information.

Problems

Problems I have designed solutions for range financing upcoming hospital appointments with external partners, to solving pain-points throughout the experience using analytic tools like MixPanel.

Being a startup transitioning to a young company has meant a lot of potential partnerships and initiatives, which has meant designing around how to implement partners into our platform without disrupting the experience.

I was not the initial designer for this product, which opened up the opportunity to give it a full visual overhaul of the experience to meet the styling and branding of the new parent company. Below is an example of how to designs looked when I joined the company.

I work with multiple product managers as well as other stakeholders to solve design challenges and deliver a smooth experience to our users.

Research

Being a platform utilized by healthcare patients, it can be challenging to interact with users. First I have to go through the healthcare provider (our customers) to find their customers (our users) who are willing to give their time for live feedback sessions.

Besides live-feedback sessions, a brief feedback survey was added to the checkout flow which has provided valuable insights which allowed tweaking of the flow.

Analytic tools like MixPanel opened up conversation with the product managers about specific points in the flow with high drop-off rates. This data allowed me to push for improved designs an A/B testing for certain screens.

On top of usability testing and analytics tools, I conducted research into adjacent products in the FinTech space such as Affirm, Shopify, Paypal, and Square.

Results

Designs that I created led to increased payment flow completion and higher NPS. Less users were dropping off at pain-points after new designs were implemented. A success story I recall is a small healthcare provider in Houston that saw their ARR increase from $100k to $233k in less than two months of implementing my designs.
