Flywire Patient Experience

What is it?

The Patient Experience is a consumer facing platform for patients to manage and pay their healthcare bills. Notifications, patient info, and insurance information are all managed in the Patient Experience.

Role

Design lead

Timeframe

March 2020-March 2023

Platform

Web

Project Summary

For over 3 years, I have been the design lead on this platform that lies at the intersection of healthcare and fintech. I worked with multiple product managers as well as other stakeholders to solve design challenges to deliver a smooth experience to our users.

The Main Problem

“Some hospitals online sites I’ve used to pay my bills are so outdated looking, you question if it’s a scam or the real thing”

-Usability testing participant

Money and medical information are very sensitive and serious topics, which requires building a lot of trust with users. It was clear that this was something many hospital payment systems were putting little emphasis on.

Low trust means less payments made, resulting in low conversion rates.

Building trust through design was a key value for this project, which many user testing subjects equated with slick, modern, and simple UI/UX.

Through conducting live usability testing sessions, I heard stories with edge cases that I wouldn't have ever expected otherwise.

“When i received the bill for my emergency room trip, I knew it was going to be high- I was not expecting seven figure balance high”

-Usability testing participant who was airlifted after a bike crash

Usability tests were also a method to confirm that design changes were indeed improvements to the user experience, rather that just running with assumptions. a brief feedback survey was added to the checkout flow which provided valuable insights which allowed tweaking of the flow.

Other Problems I faced

It is really hard to get in touch with patients

Being a platform utilized by healthcare patients, it was often challenging to interact directly with users. First I have to go through the healthcare provider (our customers) to find their customers (our users) who are willing to give their time for live feedback sessions.

Side Quests

This was a multi-year project, and I tackled many design scenarios from financing upcoming hospital appointments with external partners, to solving pain-points throughout the experience using analytic tools like MixPanel.

Being a startup transitioning to a young company has meant a lot of potential partnerships and initiatives, which has meant designing around how to implement partners into our platform without disrupting the experience.

Analytic tools like MixPanel opened up conversation with the product managers about specific points in the flow with high drop-off rates. This data allowed me to push for improved designs an A/B testing for certain screens.

On top of usability testing and analytics tools, I conducted research into adjacent products in the FinTech space such as Affirm, Shopify, Paypal, and Square.

Results

Designs that I created led to increased payment flow completion and higher NPS. Less users were dropping off at pain-points after new designs were implemented. A success story I recall is a small healthcare provider in Houston that saw their ARR increase from $100k to $233k in less than two months of implementing my designs.
