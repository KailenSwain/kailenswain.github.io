This was a solo project for my behavior class, with the goal of creating a service device that would faciliate behavior change for a user.

Duration: 5 Weeks

Skills developed:

User Research
Visual Design
Physical Prototyping
Video Production

Process

I began this project with the intent to create a device that would ehance and change the behavior of bicycle riders.

After interviewing bike users of varying skill levels, I focused my project on cyclists who ride for pleasure, rather than commuters or utilitarian riders.

Using the stories I gathered from interviews, I narrowed the problem down to a few "how might we"" statements. For these problem statements I created 30 seperate concept sheets, which I pinned up and let my peers vote on.

Digital

Starting with sketched wireframes, I created screen overlays in Adobe XD. Using Premiere I overlayed these onto a live action video that I shot to demonstrate interacting with the device.

The device is controlled through a combination button and dial, similar to the digital crown on an Apple watch. Route filtering would be done through a screen on the strava mobile app.

Physical

To create a physical prototype for filming, I first started with some sketches. Inspiration included Google Glass and the Skully AR motorcycle helmet. I kept the device seperate from the helmet for user flexibility.

I moved forward with 3D modeling in Autodesk Fusion, fabricating fast and sloppy 3D prints to test the fit and field of view. The mounting system would be a combination of rare earth magnets and 3M adhesive strips. The screens are laser cut scrap acrylic.

Deliverable

The screens and phyiscal prototype all came together in a video that I filmed and produced for the final presentation.

The device allows a cyclist to record their route, while providing realtime data such as speed and time.

Once finished, the route is then shared with other users.

The device can also be used to discover routes created by other users. Once a route is selected, the device provides a virtual path to follow, as well as performance tracking.

Upon reaching the end of the route, a brief summary of your performance is given, with more details available in the app.
