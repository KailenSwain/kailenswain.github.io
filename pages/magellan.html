Magellan: Travel Itinerary Optimization

What is it?

A streamlined app that generated an efficiently optimized trip itinerary based on points of interests and user inputs.

I conducted user interviews with some front-of-house staff to gain valuable insights into how the existing experience was fitting their needs. below are a few of the insights I gained.

Role

Owner, Designer

Timeframe

1 Week

Platform

Mobile

Project Summary

This was a passion project I started after struggling and juggling travel apps while in Asia recently. This originated from sketches I created on the 14 hour return flight and eventually translated to mockups in Figma upon returning to California.

“There’s so many extra menus and tabs- I don’t know what most of them do and easily get lost in the interface.”

-User interview participant

The existing staff experience (seem above) had too many options and screens. This made sense as the existing staff experience was built as a hyper-functional tool for all healthcare staff to use- from customer support specialist to healthcare provider admins.

A purpose-built and streamlined experience just for front desk staff would increase efficiency and make their job easier.

“I use the Manual Payments option because it can be faster to collect from a patient.”

-User interview participant

With all of its functionality, I found that front desk staff were consistently only using the staff experience to collect payments- and not even using the intended flow for collection. The more complex flow for collecting payments that was the intended method, was being used solely by phone support staff.

Insight:

The manual payments flow was all that was needed for front desk staff to collect payments.

I started with mapping out a user flow. The product manager and i decided the platform would be tablet for portability and ease of use for the staff.

The product manager and I iterated on the features needed for this experience before I started creating higher fidelity mockups.

Results

“I wish we had this sooner. It's so easy to use that anyone can pick it up without a tedious training session. There's no more confusing menus.”

-Usability testing participant

The positive responses by the interviewees confirmed that all the insights I had synthesized were correct, and that the resulting product achieved the goals of the project. To create a purpose-built and streamlined product for the front-of-house staff.
