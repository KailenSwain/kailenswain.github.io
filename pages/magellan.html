Magellan: Travel Itinerary Optimization

What is it?

A streamlined app that generated an efficiently optimized trip itinerary based on points of interests and user inputs.

I conducted user interviews with some front-of-house staff to gain valuable insights into how the existing experience was fitting their needs. below are a few of the insights I gained.

Role

Owner, Designer

Timeframe

1 Week

Platform

Mobile

Project Summary

This was a passion project I started after struggling and juggling travel apps while in Asia recently. This originated from sketches I created on the 14 hour return flight and eventually translated to mockups in Figma upon returning to California.

THe Problem

During a recent trip to Taiwan and Korea, my family and I found ourselves juggling multiple services and apps when trying to plan our free days. The following scenario actually happened:
1. Asking ChatGPT 4 or Bard for a specific type of shop near a certain neighborhood that we knew we would be near in Seoul.
2. Going to google to verify that those results actually did exist and were open (many were not actually there or open)
3. Made a list of points of interest we wanted to visit
4. Adding the POIs to a custom google map to try to eyeball the optimal routing between them based on distance.
5. Switching to a local navigation app (google maps does not work in Korea) to determine if walking or taking public transit was faster between certain POIs.

Switching between services was a huge pain, and I realized this was a design opportunity.

On the 14 hour flight back I started brainstorming on my ipad. Before landed in california I had an idea of a quick solution.

What's different from Google Maps?

While Google Maps has multi-stop functionality, it is optimized for a journey in which a users already knows all of their stops. If a users is unsure or still deciding, they have to disrupt the flow and start over to individually research a point of interest.
