Flywire Patient Portal
Time
6 months
As the product design lead for this project, I worked closely with the VP of product and multiple other stakeholders to increase online payment conversion rate with healthcare patients.

Overview

The Flywire Healthcare Patient Experience is a consumer facing platform for patients to manage and pay their healthcare bills. This is a white-label platform sold to healthcare providers as a portal for their users. Notifications, patient info, and insurance information are all managed in the Patient Experience. While a redesign might not seem significant to improve from a business standpoint, a small healthcare provider in Houston saw their ARR increase from $100k to $233k within less than two months of implementing my designs.

Role

Product design lead working closely with the VP of product and multiple PMs and stakeholders

Mission

Increasing online payment conversion rate with healthcare patients.

Timeline

6 months

Existing Product

Healthcare providers often struggle with online payments and financing options.

Money and medical information are very sensitive and serious topics, which requires building trust with users. It was clear that this was something many hospital payment systems were putting little emphasis on.

The existing product when I began this project had a very low conversion rate, around 30% completion of the user flow from initial engagement to payment reciept.

Process

Led usability tests of the existing patient experience to confirm painpoints in the experience. Through this I also discovered some crazy edge cases.

Employed the use of analytics tools including MixedPanel to do a deepdive on potential painpoints and dropoff points.

Conducted competitive analysis into adjacent products in the Fintech space such as Affirm, Shopify, Square, and Paypal.

Mapped out the existing UX to get a better understanding of the entire system.

Exploration & Collaboration

Initial exploration was done in quick white boarding sessions I facilitated with other stakeholders including product managers, engineering, and VPs.

Prototype Validation

Through social media recruiting, I facilitated usability testing of potential new designs. This qualitatively confirmed that the changes to the experience were removing pain points which would lead to increased completion rate.

Results

When the new design began to be implemented with healthcare providers the positive results were almost immediately visible.
